## Construction plan for the TE cut (§8.5), enabled by these findings

Work in the hinge frame: `h` = hinge-axis unit direction (spanwise, tilted by
twist/dihedral), `a` = chordwise-aft unit direction (global +X projected
perpendicular to `h`), `u = h × a`. Hinge point on the camber line at
`hinge_xc`.

Two nested cylinders about the hinge axis + an aft half-space box give **exact
volume conservation by construction** (OML partitions into three disjoint
sets):

- `nose_cyl` = cylinder radius `R_nose` about the hinge axis — the control
  surface's rounded nose (the "revolution about the hinge axis" of §8.5), so it
  can rotate. Sized `R_nose = 0.6 × min local half-thickness at hinge_xc` over
  the CS span, so the nose stays inside the OML.
- `cove_cyl` = cylinder radius `R_cove = R_nose + gap_mm` — the wing's concave
  cove recess. Distinct radius ⇒ nose/cove arcs are concentric-but-not-equal ⇒
  **never tangent** (dodges F4); the gap_mm is the radial clearance.
- `aft_box` = large box on the +`a` side of the hinge-axis plane = "everything
  aft of the hinge".

Bodies:
- **CS** = OML ∩ (`nose_cyl` ∪ `aft_box`) over the INSET span
  `[y0+gap, y1-gap]` — rounded nose + aft body, held clear of the wing at the
  spanwise ends.
- **wing** = OML − `cove_cyl` − `aft_box` over the FULL span `[y0, y1]` — keeps
  its fixed structure outboard/inboard of the device, with a cove recess.
- **gap** = OML − wing − CS (the annular clearance + the two spanwise end
  gaps). Since CS ⊆ (removed-from-wing region), wing ∩ CS = ∅ and
  vol(wing)+vol(CS)+vol(gap) = vol(OML) exactly (modulo kernel fuzz) — this is
  the §9 P4 conservation criterion, satisfied by construction rather than luck.

Scoping: P4/P5 gate configs are half-wing (`mirror: false`) so the body counts
match the plan (TE ⇒ exactly 2 bodies; +LE droop ⇒ 3 in P5). Mirroring devices
onto both wings is the same cut applied twice and is deferred.

## Refinement: per-station arc construction replaces the cylinder cove (§8.5)

The cylinder-cove construction above conserved volume exactly but used a
uniform gap_mm radial clearance and an ad-hoc nose-radius heuristic
(`TE_NOSE_RADIUS_FRACTION`) — not a real tangency condition to the skin, and
brittle when a requested clearance didn't fit the local half-thickness (the
CS split into 2 bodies at gap_mm=5mm on a thin station). Replaced with a
normative per-station construction: sections perpendicular to the hinge axis,
arcs centered on the axis through the "normal foot" on each skin (which is
tangent to the skin by construction — no solver), and a wing-side cove arc at
the same center offset by a fixed `COVE_CLEARANCE_MM` (5 mm), never tangent to
the nose. Verified below before implementing.

## Construction (implemented)

Rather than hand-build each wing/CS cross-section wire (skin arc + cove arc +
fillet, trimmed and stitched per station — fragile and hard to get watertight
across ~20+ varying stations), the per-station arc profiles are used to build
**lofted region solids** (a nose-region and a cove-region, each a loft of the
per-station axis-centered arc profiles, tapered along the span since Ru/Rl
vary per station), which then take the SAME ROLE the P4-v1 cylinders did in
the boolean composition (`CS = OML ∩ (nose_region ∪ aft_box)`,
`wing = OML − cove_region − aft_box`). This keeps the proven exact-volume-
conservation boolean algebra while satisfying the normative per-station
tangency/clearance construction: within any station's cross-sectional plane,
the nose is exactly a circle (or 2-arc blend) centered on the hinge axis with
radius fixed by that station's normal feet, and the cove is a concentric
circle offset by exactly `COVE_CLEARANCE_MM` — so the clearance is invariant
under rotation about the axis (i.e. at any deflection angle) BY CONSTRUCTION,
not just at 0°. The lead-in fillet is applied as a 3D edge fillet
(`Solid.fillet`) on the wing body's cove-to-skin transition edge post-boolean.

## probe_brep_cache.py (geometry build cache, test-architecture fix)

Full-gate runs were being used as the diagnostic loop for a construction
question (te_half_twisted timing) — a ~263s round trip per hypothesis.
Fixing this needs a disk cache for the expensive boolean outputs so repeated
gate runs against unchanged config+code don't rebuild. Verified before
implementing (never assume a serialization API):

- `cadquery` 2.8.0 (installed) has first-class `cq.Shape.exportBrep(path)` /
  `cq.Shape.importBrep(path)` — no need to drop to raw `OCP.BRepTools`.
- Single-solid round-trip: volume preserved exactly (6000.0000 -> 6000.0000).
- Multi-solid Compound round-trip (2 disjoint solids, standing in for a raw
  pre-shard-filter boolean output): solid count preserved (2->2), each
  solid's volume preserved exactly (0.125, 1000.0).
- CONCLUSION: safe to cache the RAW `wing_shape`/`cs_shape` Compounds
  `fuzzy_cut`/`fuzzy_common` produce, *before* `filter_shards` runs, and
  re-run `filter_shards` + sort + gap-volume arithmetic fresh on every load
  (cache-hit or miss) — only the expensive OCC boolean is skipped on a hit;
  every assertion still runs on freshly-derived properties of the loaded
  shape, never on a cached scalar.

## te_half_twisted timing investigation (post cache/instrumentation)

Per-stage timings (artifacts/gates/p04_timings.json, cold cache) resolved
the open question of whether te_half_twisted's earlier ">150s, no
completion" observation was a hang: it is not — it completes in ~145s
(construction) / ~470s (full 8-test gate incl. a forced-fresh slow-tier
rebuild), comfortably inside the 600s per-test budget. The 2-4x cost vs the
untwisted config is real and isolated to `BRepAlgoAPI_Cut`/`Common`
themselves (lofting and station analysis are identical between configs) —
see docs/known_issues.md ("Twisted/tilted device configs cost 2-4x more per
boolean than untwisted") for the full breakdown and why no construction
change was applied.
