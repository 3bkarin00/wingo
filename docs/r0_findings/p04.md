## Construction plan for the TE cut (§8.5), enabled by these findings

Work in the hinge frame: `h` = hinge-axis unit direction (spanwise, tilted by
twist/dihedral), `a` = chordwise-aft unit direction (global +X projected
perpendicular to `h`), `u = h × a`. Hinge point on the camber line at
`hinge_xc`.

Two nested cylinders about the hinge axis + an aft half-space box give **exact
volume conservation by construction** (OML partitions into three disjoint
sets):

- `nose_cyl` = cylinder radius `R_nose` about the hinge axis — the control
  surface's rounded nose (the "revolution about the hinge axis" of §8.5), so it
  can rotate. Sized `R_nose = 0.6 × min local half-thickness at hinge_xc` over
  the CS span, so the nose stays inside the OML.
- `cove_cyl` = cylinder radius `R_cove = R_nose + gap_mm` — the wing's concave
  cove recess. Distinct radius ⇒ nose/cove arcs are concentric-but-not-equal ⇒
  **never tangent** (dodges F4); the gap_mm is the radial clearance.
- `aft_box` = large box on the +`a` side of the hinge-axis plane = "everything
  aft of the hinge".

Bodies:
- **CS** = OML ∩ (`nose_cyl` ∪ `aft_box`) over the INSET span
  `[y0+gap, y1-gap]` — rounded nose + aft body, held clear of the wing at the
  spanwise ends.
- **wing** = OML − `cove_cyl` − `aft_box` over the FULL span `[y0, y1]` — keeps
  its fixed structure outboard/inboard of the device, with a cove recess.
- **gap** = OML − wing − CS (the annular clearance + the two spanwise end
  gaps). Since CS ⊆ (removed-from-wing region), wing ∩ CS = ∅ and
  vol(wing)+vol(CS)+vol(gap) = vol(OML) exactly (modulo kernel fuzz) — this is
  the §9 P4 conservation criterion, satisfied by construction rather than luck.

Scoping: P4/P5 gate configs are half-wing (`mirror: false`) so the body counts
match the plan (TE ⇒ exactly 2 bodies; +LE droop ⇒ 3 in P5). Mirroring devices
onto both wings is the same cut applied twice and is deferred.
